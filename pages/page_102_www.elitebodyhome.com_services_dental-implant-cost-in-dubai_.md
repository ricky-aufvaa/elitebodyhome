## What Are Dental Implants?
Dental implants are recent and until now the most scientifically approved method for replacing missing teeth. Unlike traditional dentures or bridges, which rest on the gums, implants are implanted into the jawbone just like natural teeth.
## Understanding the Dental Implant Procedure ‚Äî A Step by Step Guide
If you are a patient, knowing what to expect for the dental implant process may ease your mind. Here is a rundown of the steps involved:
##### Assessment and Initial Consultation
We start with an extensive consultation and physical examination. We use advanced imaging techniques like 3D X-rays to assess the condition of your jawbone and the placement of the missing tooth or teeth.
##### Placement of the Implant
We then carry out the implant procedure once the treatment plan is decided upon. We do this under local anesthesia, and make sure you are comfortable and pain-free.
##### Healing and Osseointegration
The implant itself needs time to bond with the bone, which is called osseointegration. Depending on the case selected the complete process can be closed in short span of time, although the whole thing can too months to complete.
##### Abutment and Crown/Bridge Placement
Once the implant is integrated, placed abutment and final crown/bridge.
##### Aftercare
Success of dental implants depends greatly on proper aftercare. It is essential that you practice good oral hygiene, come in for regular check-ups, and follow all post-surgery instructions given to you by our team.
## Why Choose Dental Implants?
##### Aesthetic Appeal
Dental implants look and feel just like your natural teeth. Their custom crowns are color matched so that they look like they belong to your smile.
##### Improved Function
Unlike dentures, which can move around or be a sore spot, dental implants are secure in the jawbone. This means that you can chew, talk and smile with confidence.
##### No Damage to Adjacent Teeth
Implants are purely individual teeth similar to your natural teeth whereas crowns & bridges on the other hand requires grinding of adjacent teeth during placements.
##### Longevity of Duration
When cared for appropriately, dental implants can last a lifetime. Unlike bridges or dentures, which must be replaced every few years.
##### High Success Rate
A scientifically backed and well done dental implant can have a 97-98% success rate among healthy people.
