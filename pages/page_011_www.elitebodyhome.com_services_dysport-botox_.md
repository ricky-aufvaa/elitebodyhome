## What is Dysport Botox?
Botox and Dysport are two well-liked nonsurgical wrinkle-reduction procedures. Although they both target the muscles that result in fine lines, their approved applications differ in a few ways. The wrinkles between the eyebrows are known as glabellar lines, and Dysport is particularly licensed to treat these. Contrarily, Botox is authorized for a broader range of regions, such as glabellar lines, crow's feet (lines surrounding the eyes), and forehead wrinkles. Although their focal regions are slightly different, both treatments relax muscles to produce smoother skin.
## Steps For Dysport Botox Treatment Procedure
By relaxing specific muscles, the Dysport Botox method is a non-invasive way to temporarily lessen the appearance of wrinkles on the face. The procedure smoothes out the skin on top by injecting a tiny quantity of botulinum toxin (Dysport) into particular muscles to stop them from contracting.

## Preparation for Dysport Botox
Being well-prepared is essential to getting the most out of your Dysport Botox treatment. The following actions should be taken before to the procedure:
##### Steer clear of blood thinners
To reduce bruising, avoid taking aspirin, ibuprofen, or other blood-thinning drugs at least one week before the procedure.
##### Keep hydrated
To keep your skin hydrated and healthy, drink a lot of water in the days before your visit.
##### Limit alcohol consumption
To lower the chance of bruising and swelling, abstain from alcohol for 24 hours before your treatment.
##### Notify your practitioner
Some medications or supplements may interfere with the process, so let your provider know about them.
##### Come with Clean Skin
On the day of your appointment, show up with a clean face devoid of lotions or makeup.
