## Procedure
Wart removal is a process that removes warts by multiple techniques, depending on the size, type, and location of the wart. ##### Cryotherapy

##### Salicylic Acid Treatment

##### Surgical Excision
A surgery is performed to cut the wart, which is a method that is done in large or persistent cases under local anesthesia.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Does wart removal hurt?
Wart removal procedures, like cryotherapy or laser removal, may cause mild discomfort or a stinging sensation, but numbing options are available to reduce pain. Most patients report only minimal pain.
### How long does the wart removal process take?
The process is usually quick, lasting 15-30 minutes depending on the size and number of warts being treated. Some warts may require multiple sessions for complete removal.
### Will the wart come back after treatment?
While most treatments effectively remove warts, HPV remains in the body, so new warts can appear. Proper aftercare and follow-up treatments help minimize recurrence.
### Are there any side effects?
Minor side effects may include redness, swelling, or a blister at the treatment site. These are temporary and usually resolve within a few days.
### Is wart removal safe?
Yes, wart removal is generally safe when performed by trained professionals. Our clinicians follow strict safety protocols to minimize risks and ensure effective results.
