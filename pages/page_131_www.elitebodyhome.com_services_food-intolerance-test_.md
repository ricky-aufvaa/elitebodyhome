## Procedure
The food intolerance test includes several methods to detect the body's sensitivity to certain types of foods, the most common being blood tests, skin tests, or elimination diets. These methods help identify foods that may cause symptoms such as bloating, diarrhea, headaches, or fatigue.
##### Initial Consultation
Discussion of symptoms, eating habits and health concerns with a specialist doctor.
##### Blood Sample Collection
A simple blood test is required to measure IgG antibody levels against specific foods.
##### Laboratory Analysis
The sample is examined to check the body's reaction to different groups of foods and food additives.
##### Doctor's Review
A consultation session with the doctor to review the results and develop a customized nutritional plan based on the tests.
##### Health Report
You receive a comprehensive report showing the types of foods that are likely to cause sensitivity or intolerance.
##### Ongoing Support
You'll receive ongoing nutritional guidance to help modify your diet and manage intolerance cases effectively.
## Benefits
A food intolerance test can make a big difference in improving your quality of life by identifying foods that cause you discomfort or health problems. Here are the main benefits this test offers:
##### Customized Nutritional Guidance
The results help design a diet that avoids foods that cause you allergies, reducing digestive disorders.
##### Improved Digestion
You can enjoy better digestion and reduce annoying symptoms like bloating, gas and indigestion by avoiding foods that upset the digestive system.
##### Increased Energy Levels
Food intolerance cases may cause fatigue, and modifying the diet helps boost activity and vitality throughout the day.
##### Relief from Chronic Symptoms
Symptoms of intolerance to some foods sometimes appear as headaches, skin problems, or joint pain, and identifying and excluding these foods from the diet helps get rid of these persistent symptoms.
## Preparation
Preparing for a food intolerance test is easy and simple, and requires following just a few steps to ensure accurate results. Here are the guidelines you should follow before taking the test:
##### Continue your usual diet
Make sure to eat the foods you usually eat in the days leading up to the test, so that the results reflect your body's actual response to food.
##### Avoid certain medications
Some medications may affect test results. Consult your doctor to find out if you should stop any medications before the test.
##### Stay hydrated
Drink enough water before the test, as it helps facilitate blood sample collection and ensures the body is in a normal state during the test.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How accurate are food intolerance tests?
Our IgG antibody tests have high accuracy rates when properly conducted and interpreted by our specialists in conjunction with your symptoms.
### What's the difference between food allergy and intolerance?
Food allergies involve the immune system and can be life-threatening, while intolerances typically cause digestive discomfort and other non-life-threatening symptoms.
### How long do I need to eliminate problem foods?
This varies by individual, but many people see improvement within 2-6 weeks of eliminating problematic foods identified in testing.
### Can children take food intolerance tests?
Yes, we offer testing for children aged 3 and above who may be experiencing food-related symptoms.
