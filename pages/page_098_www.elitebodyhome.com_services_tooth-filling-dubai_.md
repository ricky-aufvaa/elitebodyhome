## Post-Dental Filling Care
##### Eating After Procedure
Eat only after the numbness wears off, or you risk taking a bite out of the wrong thing.
##### Chewing Precautions
Do not chew on the side with the new filling for 24 hours, especially with metal amalgam fillings.
##### Sensitivity Management
You may experience some sensitivity to hot, cold or sweet foods; toothpaste for sensitive teeth can help if that happens.
##### Oral Hygiene
Be sure to brush twice a day, and floss gently around the filling, not brushing too hard.
##### Food Restrictions
Limit hard or sticky foods that may chip or dislodge the filling, especially immediately after the procedure.
##### Follow-up Care
If the filling feels rough or your bite feels uneven, call the dentist for an adjustment.
##### Regular Check-ups
Regular dental check-ups will help in evaluating the condition of the filling, as well as ensure oral health.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### Is tooth extraction painful?
Local anesthesia or sedation ensures the procedure is painless. You may experience mild discomfort afterward, but it is manageable with over-the-counter pain medications.
### How long does it take to recover from a tooth extraction?
Most patients recover within 1-2 weeks. For surgical extractions, recovery may take a bit longer, especially for impacted wisdom teeth.
### What can I eat after a tooth extraction?
Soft foods such as yogurt, mashed potatoes, and smoothies are recommended for the first few days. Avoid chewing near the extraction site to promote healing.
### Can I brush my teeth after an extraction?
You can brush your teeth, but avoid the extraction site for the first 24 hours to prevent dislodging the blood clot. After 24 hours, gently rinse your mouth with salt water.
