## What is a PDO Thread Lift?
Threads are made of polydioxanone, which has been used in surgery for many years. The sutures are inserted beneath the skin using very thin needles, and the sutures dissolve over time naturally, promoting collagen synthesis and providing better skin texture, elasticity and firmness. Unlike traditional facelifts, a PDO Thread Lift can be performed without large incisions or extensive recovery time. The threads raise droopy spots, smooth out wrinkles and provide the face with a fresher look.
## PDO thread lift process Procedure for PDO Thread Lift
What Is The PDO Thread Lift Treatment? A PDO Thread Lift is a fast and non-invasive procedure that can be done in the office under local anesthesia. Here's a step-by-step rundown of how the process generally goes:
##### Consultation
An extensive consultation takes place to evaluate the areas needing treatment and to ensure the patient's desired outcomes are in line with PDO Thread Lift expectations.
##### Anesthesia
Once you are comfortable, local anesthesia is injected to eliminate any pain throughout the surgery.
##### Insertion of Threads
PDO threads are introduced into the subdermal layer of the skin with the help of an ultra-fine needle in the targeted areas.
##### Lifting and Contouring
The threads are strategically adjusted to hold up drooping skin and reshape the face. It can be carefully placed in the cheeks, jawline, neck, or brow area.

## Am I a Good Candidate for PDO Thread Lift?
The PDO Thread Lift provides a perfect solution for those wanting moderate lifting of their skin without having to go under the knife. However, it's not right for everyone.
##### Mild to Moderate Skin Laxity
It's best for those who have early sagging in areas such as your cheeks, jowls, neck or brow.
##### Good Skin Elasticity
Patient must have reasonably good skin health.
##### Realistic Expectations
Candidates should know that this is a non-surgical procedure, and thus the results may not be as dramatic as those of a full facelift.
##### No Significant Medical Conditions
PDO Thread Lifts are not recommended for those with autoimmune conditions and a history of poor wound healing.
## What To Do To Prepare For A PDO Thread Lift
Pre-op is very important in getting good results and an uneventful recovery. Your physician will give you individualized instructions for your PDO Thread Lift.
##### Medications that Thin the Blood

##### Limit Alcohol Consumption
Do not consume alcohol for 48 hours prior to your appointment, as this can increase the risk of bruising and swelling.
##### Hydrate
Be sure to drink plenty of fluids in the days before the procedure.
##### Plan for Downtime
Since recovery is minimal, most people will experience swelling or bruising, so plan to take it easy for 24‚Äì48 hours after the treatment.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How Long Does a PDO Thread Lift Last?
The results of a PDO Thread Lift can last anywhere from 12 to 18 months, depending on factors such as age, lifestyle, and skin type. The collagen-stimulating effects continue even after the threads dissolve.
### Is the procedure painful?
Most patients report minimal discomfort due to the use of local anesthesia. You may experience slight soreness or tightness post-procedure, which typically subsides within a few days.
### How soon can I see the results?
You will see some immediate lifting, with final results becoming more evident over the following weeks as collagen production increases.
### Are there any side effects?
Common side effects include minor swelling, bruising, and tenderness in the treated areas. These typically subside within a few days. Rarely, complications such as infection or thread displacement may occur.
