# Spider Veins Treatment
Spider veins, with its medical term as telangiectasias, are small, weakened veins that show close to the surface of the skin. They're typically thin, red or blue-thread veins that appear on your leg or face. Although they're harmless, spider veins can be painful and make you feel self-conscious. Lucky for you, there are treatments that are proven to help eliminate or reduce the appearance of spider veins.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### What causes spider veins?
Spider veins can be caused by factors such as aging, hormonal changes, genetics, pregnancy, and prolonged standing or sitting. Over time, veins weaken, leading to the development of spider veins.
### Is the treatment painful?
Most patients experience minimal discomfort during spider vein treatments. Sclerotherapy may cause mild burning or stinging during the injection, while laser therapy may cause a slight tingling sensation. Anesthesia is not usually necessary.
### How long does recovery take?
Recovery time is generally short. Patients can typically return to normal activities within a day or two after treatment. However, strenuous activities and prolonged sun exposure should be avoided for several weeks.
### How many sessions are required?
The number of sessions depends on the size and number of veins being treated. Most people require between 1-3 sessions for optimal results.
