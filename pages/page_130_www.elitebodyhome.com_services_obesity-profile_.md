## What's in Our Obesity Profile
A multitude of factors can impact metabolism, so a thorough evaluation should aid in diagnosing potential metabolic disorders, nutritional deficiencies, and obesity-related risk factors, allowing healthcare providers to craft individualized modes of treatment.
## Procedure
Obesity Profile: This is a battery of laboratory examinations and clinical evaluations which reflect the obesity subject's general condition and risk factors. These include hormone testing, metabolic testing, and other tests that can reveal underlying issues that may be contributing to weight gain or weight loss resistance.
##### Initial Consultation
An extensive discussion with a physician about your medical history, lifestyle, and weight-related concerns to customize the profile.
##### Body Composition Analysis
Get a complete picture of your weight composition. Assess values such as your body mass index (BMI), fat, muscle mass, and other indicators to understand how you are building weight.
##### Laboratory Tests
Blood tests to measure cholesterol, blood sugar, thyroid function and other obesity-related markers.
##### Metabolic Assessment
Identify your metabolic rate and energy expenditure to know how well your body burns calories.
##### Nutritional Assessment
Sit down with a nutritionist to study your dietary intake and lifestyle and find gaps or patterns of poor eating.
##### Physical Activity Evaluation
Consider your exercise routine and how often you are active; you must balance your exercise with what you eat for it's impact on your weight.
##### Doctor's Review and Recommendations
After all data is collected, a physician will review the results and recommend a personalized weight management program, including diet, exercise and lifestyle changes.
##### Follow-Up Plan
Long-term follow-up sessions to track progress and revision of the plan as necessary.

## Preparation
It is essential to go through the right preparations to be well prepared for an Obesity Profile test to get accurate results.
##### Fasting
Diverse blood investigations, especially those assessing glucose and lipids levels, need fasting for 8 to 12 hours before the test.
##### Medication Disclosure
You should inform your healthcare provider about any medications or supplements you are taking, because some may affect the results.
##### Avoid Strenuous Exercise
Avoid strenuous activity for 24 hours prior to testing to reduce chances that certain hormonal or metabolic tests may be affected.
##### Hydration
Drink plenty of water so blood tests are more easily carried out and help to make them accurate.
## Results
Your Obesity Profile provides specific insights into the underlying causes of obesity and includes actionable insights to help you determine appropriate treatment.
##### Metabolic Health
The results will clarify whether metabolic disorders like insulin resistance or thyroid dysfunction are at the root of your weight problems.
##### Nutritional Deficiencies
It identifies key vitamin and mineral deficiency that are possibly preventing weight loss or boosting energy.
##### Hormonal Imbalances
Identifies abnormalities in hormones like cortisol, leptin or thyroid hormones, that can directly influence control of weight.

## Frequently Asked Questions
### How long does the obesity profile testing take?
The complete assessment typically takes 2-3 hours including consultations and tests, with lab results available within 3-5 business days.
### Will this test diagnose why I can't lose weight?
Yes, the profile is specifically designed to identify metabolic, hormonal and nutritional factors that may be contributing to weight loss resistance.
### Do I need to stop medications before testing?
Some medications may need to be paused before testing - your doctor will provide specific instructions during your consultation.
### How often should I repeat the obesity profile?
We recommend repeating the profile every 6-12 months to monitor progress and adjust treatment plans as needed.
