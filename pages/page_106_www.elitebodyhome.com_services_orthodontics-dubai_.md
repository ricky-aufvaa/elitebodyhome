## Process Of Orthodontic treatment procedure
A teeth straightener is a dental device used to align your teeth through orthodontic treatment which realigns your teeth using braces or aligners over a period. It is a well-planned procedure that provides long-lasting effect making your smile healthy.
##### Initial Consultation
An orthodontist will evaluate your teeth, take X-rays and consult with you on your specific concerns and goals.
##### Customized Treatment Plan
A treatment plan is customized according to your needs and may include braces, clear aligners, or other devices.
##### Fitting and Adjustments
They, then, fit braces or aligners, with regular adjustments to ensure progress.
##### Monitoring and Maintenance
The orthodontist periodically adjusts the aligners to ensure that teeth are tracking properly and moving into their intended positions.
## Benefits
Orthodontic treatment benefits go far beyond just a pretty smile. Advantages functional and health:
##### Improved Oral Health
Correctly aligned teeth are simpler to clean, which lowers the chance of cavities, gum disease and more.
##### Better Functionality
When your teeth are properly aligned, it can improve your bite and lessen the risk of jaw pain, TMJ disorders, and chewing problems.
##### Enhanced Self-Esteem
A dazzling smile can improve one with regards to confidence and all prosperity.
##### Long-Term Stability
With correctly positioned teeth, the likelihood of orthodontic work is also lessened in future.
## Eligibility
Orthodontics can be done at a young age or later in life, and the correction of problems at a younger age is often more beneficial. But many adults receive orthodontic treatment as well, even later in life.
##### Children
Intervening early, typically at the age of 7, could avoid more serious problems later on; by then, teeth and the jaw are still growing.
##### Dental Health
Candidates also need to have healthy gums and teeth because conditions like gum disease or cavities must be treated prior to beginning treatment.
##### Jaw Alignment Issues
People who have bite problems, such as overbite, underbite, crossbite, or open bite, are good candidates to receive orthodontic treatment.
##### Crowding or Spacing Problems
Orthodontics can be beneficial for patients with crowded, crooked teeth or for those with excessive space between their teeth.
## Preparation
There are multiple ways you can prepare your teeth for upcoming orthodontic treatment. If everything is well-prepared, treatment can be streamlined and better results can be achieved in a shorter period of time.
##### Consultation and Diagnosis
At your first appointment, the orthodontist will discuss your dental history, take X-rays and develop a treatment plan.
##### Oral Hygiene Routine
Orthodontic appliances may hinder the cleaning process, so develop a strict oral care routine from the beginning.
##### Dietary Adjustments
Do not eat anything sticky or hard that can damage the braces or disrupt the treatment.
##### Regular Dental Checkups
Get your teeth and gums healthy before applying for orthodontic treatment.
